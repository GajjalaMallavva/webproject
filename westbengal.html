BEST WATERFALLS IN WEST BENGAL

Changey waterfalls

This stunning waterfall, known as Changey Falls, can be seen close to Kolakham in the Kalimpong District of West Bengal. The Kalimpong area includes some of the most beautiful waterfalls in all of Lava. Set amongst rolling hills and dense woods lies the little yet beautiful Changey Falls.The waterfall sits at an elevation of 2,200 m (7,218 ft) and is situated at a distance of about 4 km (2.5 mi) from Kolakham and 10 km (6.2 mi) from the hamlet of Lava in Kalimpong district, West Bengal. It is reachable via a 900 m (0.56 mi) trek along a short walking trail from the last motorable point. The waterfall has several viewing decks.

Ghagra waterfalls

Ghagra is about 7 km north from Belpahari. It is actually a waterfall passing through a canyon of black stone. The word “Ghagra” came from the word “gagra” which means a Kalash filledup with water. Actually the shape of the canyon is like a kalash. Tharafeni dam is very closed to Ghagra.Tarafeni and Ghagra waterfallTarafeni Barrage under Belpahari Gram Panchayat is definitely an more attractive place where public get enthralled at the sight of atractive nature and of the river currents against the rocky boulders, during rainy season. It is at a distance of nearly 5 k.m.away from Belpahari.

Chunnu waterfalls

One of the major attractions of Darjeeling, the main highlight of this garden is the Chunnu summer falls. Built by the Gorkha Hill Council Tourism Department, this place was named the rock garden because the entire place is made out of cutting rocks from different levels. Chunnu Summer falls can be reached from Darjeeling via rented cars from Darjeeling town. It is approximately 10 kilometers from the town of Darjeeling. While proceeding downhill from Darjeeling via the Hill Cart Road, one has to turn right, a little before reaching Ghoom Station. Albeit the road is narrow and descends rapidly into the valley, after you pass across a few households and a couple of sharp turns into the valley you would be in for surreal landscapes .
